## What is AWS Academy?

[AWS Academy](https://aws.amazon.com/training/awsacademy/) is Amazon’s program for educators and students. It offers ready-made curricula, labs, and knowledge checks so you can learn AWS in a structured way. Courses are delivered through the **AWS Academy Learning Management System** (Canvas).

- [AWS Academy – Training and Certification](https://aws.amazon.com/training/awsacademy/)
- [AWS Academy FAQs](https://aws.amazon.com/training/awsacademy/faq/)

## How to log in

1. **Get an invite**  
   I will send you an **invitation to the course** via your **@scranton.edu** email address. Check that inbox (and spam) for a message from AWS Academy.

2. **Use your University email**  
   You will log in to the AWS Academy learning portal using your **University of Scranton email** (e.g. `yourname@scranton.edu`). Do not use a personal email; the course is tied to the address on the invite.

3. **Open the learning portal**  
   Go to **[https://awsacademy.instructure.com](https://awsacademy.instructure.com)** (or use the link in the invitation email). Sign in with the same credentials you use for your @scranton.edu account, or follow the instructions in the invite (e.g. set a password or use SSO if your institution uses it).

4. **Accept the course**  
   From the invite or the portal dashboard, open the course and accept the enrollment if prompted. After that, you can access modules, lectures, labs, and knowledge checks.

If you did not receive an invite or cannot log in, contact me.

## Course structure at a glance

The course is split into **10 modules**. Each module typically includes:

- **Lecture** — Video or text covering the concepts.
- **Activity / Lab / Demo** — Hands-on exercises (e.g. in the AWS console or guided labs).
- **Knowledge Check** — Short quiz to reinforce the material.
